Tomato Sauce

Description

This is a recipe which I, Kyle Fingerhut have developed over years as a seasoned chef. I have experimented diligently to perfect this recipe so you don't have to.

This recipe is suitable for one or many people and can be freely combined with the noodle of your choice (though I do prefer rigatoni).

Ingredients:

  • four tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil
  • three cloves of garlic, thinly sliced
  • one medium sized white onion, diced
  • two 12 ounce cans of Italian tomatoes
  • two tablespoons of tomato paste
  • two tablespoons of Italian spice mix
  • a sprig or two of fresh basil, chopped
  • salt, to taste
  • red pepper flakes, to taste
  • optional: teaspoon of sugar

Steps:

  1. Add the oil to a deep-sided pan over medium heat.
  2. Once the oil is slick, add the garlic and onion along with a dash of salt. Saut√© for roughly five minutes until the onions become transluscent and fragrant.
  3. Add the tomatoes, tomato paste, spices, and red pepper flakes. Bring to a boil.
  4. Once boiling, immediately lower to a simmer. Let simmer for 30 minutes to an hour.
  5. Remove from heat and transfer to a blender. Pulse until the sauce reaches the desired texture.
  6. Transfer back to the pot and add the fresh basil once the sauce has slightly cooled.
  7. Taste and, if acidic, add sugar to cut the acidity. Add salt and red pepper flakes to taste.
